Mark Zuckerberg

Mark Zuckerberg is an American businessman and philanthropist, best known as the co-founder of Facebook. Born on May 14, 1984, in White Plains, New York, Zuckerberg developed an interest in computers at a young age. He left Harvard University after his sophomore year to focus on Facebook, which he launched in 2004 with his friends. Zuckerberg quickly became one of the world's youngest billionaires as Facebook's user base grew exponentially. He is also known for his philanthropic efforts, including the establishment of the Chan Zuckerberg Initiative. Zuckerberg is married to Priscilla Chan, a pediatrician, and they have two daughters

Education

Mark Zuckerberg's education journey began at Ardsley High School before transferring to Phillips Exeter Academy in New Hampshire for his high school education. He later enrolled at Harvard University in 2002, where he studied psychology and computer science. Zuckerberg's time at Harvard was marked by his exceptional coding skills and the development of programs like CourseMatch and Facemash. However, he decided to drop out of Harvard during his sophomore year to focus on Facebook, the social networking site he co-founded with his friends, which quickly grew to become a global phenomenon

       

Carrier

Mark Zuckerberg is the founder, chairman, and CEO of Meta, formerly known as Facebook, which he established in 2004. He is responsible for setting the overall direction and product strategy for the company, overseeing its growth and evolution into a global social networking platform Zuckerberg's career began with the launch of Facebook while he was a student at Harvard University in 2004. His innovative approach to social networking quickly propelled Facebook to unprecedented success, making him one of the world's youngest billionaires Despite controversies and challenges, Zuckerberg has steered Meta through various transformations, including the rebranding to Meta in 2021, emphasizing the development of social experiences for the metaverse through immersive technologies like augmented and virtual reality

Awards

Mark Zuckerberg has not won a Nobel Peace Prize. While there were speculations in 2011 that he might be a contender for the award, the Peace Prize that year was actually awarded to President Ellen Johnson Sirleaf of Liberia
