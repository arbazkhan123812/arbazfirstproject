Shahid Afridi

Shahid Afridi, also known as Sahibzada Mohammad Shahid Khan Afridi, is a former Pakistani cricketer and captain of the Pakistan national cricket team. He is renowned for his explosive batting style and leg-spin bowling.

Education

Shahid Afridi attended Government Islamia Science College for his education.

Carrier

Afridi made his ODI debut in 1996 and is famously known for scoring the fastest century in ODI cricket off just 37 balls in his debut innings. Throughout his career, he was a dynamic all-rounder, excelling in both batting and bowling. Afridi established the Shahid Afridi Foundation in 2014

Awards

Shahid Afridi has received several awards for his contributions to cricket and philanthropy. He was awarded the Pride of Performance by the President of Pakistan Asif Ali Zardari on March 23, 2010, and the Sitara-i-Imtiaz by the President of Pakistan Mamnoon Hussain on March 23, 2018
