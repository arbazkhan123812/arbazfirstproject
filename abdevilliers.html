Ab De VIlliers

AB de Villiers, whose full name is Abraham Benjamin de Villiers, is a retired South African cricketer born on February 17, 1984, in Warmbad, South Africa. Known for his exceptional talent and innovation in cricket, he is considered one of the most versatile and skilled batsmen in the history of the sport. De Villiers made his international debut for South Africa in December 2004 and went on to have a successful career, scoring over 20,000 runs across all formats of the game. He holds several records, including being the fastest batsman to reach 8,000, 9,000, and 10,000 runs in One Day Internationals, as well as the fastest fifty, hundred, and 150 in ODI cricket. Apart from his batting prowess, he was also recognized for his fielding skills and occasional wicketkeeping. De Villiers retired from international cricket in May 2018 to focus on his family and other commitments but continued to play in various T20 leagues globally, such as the Indian Premier League (IPL) and the Caribbean Premier League (CPL). Throughout his career, AB de Villiers earned a reputation as one of the greatest cricketers of all time, leaving a lasting legacy in the sport

Education

AB de Villiers did his schooling at Afrikaans Boys' High School in Pretoria, South Africa. Alongside cricket, he excelled in various sports such as golf, rugby, swimming, and others during his school days
